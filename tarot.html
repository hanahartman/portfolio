About Tarot

Tarot is an ancient system of divination through a 78-card deck of symbolic images. However, no modern tarot reader worth their salt will claim to be able to predict the future with 100% accuracy or to have all the answers about life and destiny. As one resource explains:

"In most people's minds, "Tarot card reading" means a woman in flowing robes, leaning over a small table in a candlelit room, foretelling impending doom.

But that's not really what Tarot cards are about. In fact, they're not even really meant to tell your fortune or future. According to The Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn, "The most powerful sources of information come from within; the Tarot aids in coming in contact with one's Higher Self."" [source]

Essentially, the value of a tarot reading is up to you, the querent. When traditional methods of thinking about your life don't seem to help, Tarot can be a valuable source of guidance. Whether you're making a difficult decision, feeling lost, concerned about your future, or simply need a fresh perspective, a Tarot reading might be the option you've been looking for.

My Approach

As a reader, I focus on the issues that are most important to you as the querent. I take the time to get to know you and the many factors in your life that may influence a reading's interpretation. My goal is to help you get the most from the cards by searching out the most helpful advice the cards can offer for your unique struggles.

Be prepared, however, that in choosing me as a reader you will not get a generic or standardized result that could fit any situation: your reading may uncover difficult truths that get to the heart of the situation, whether or not they are comfortable or the advice that you would want to hear.
